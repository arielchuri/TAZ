## **1\. Elevator Pitch**

Named after Hakim Bey's radical philosophy, Temporary Autonomous Zone (TAZ) OS is a localized, lightweight operating system designed to run entirely off an offline, solar-powered Raspberry Pi mesh network \[cite: 1, 2\]. It replaces top-down municipal management with horizontal mutual aid, providing everyday people with the immediate digital infrastructure to distribute food, coordinate medical defense, and make direct collective decisions without a central hierarchy when traditional systems fail or retreat \[cite: 1, 2\].

## **2\. How the Community Works**

The community created by this tool operates on the principles of Mutualism and Anarcho-Syndicalism \[cite: 1, 2\]. When a crisis occurs, a temporary autonomous zone forms to operate as a Mutual Aid Network \[cite: 1, 2\].

* **Property and Public Goods:** People maintain their personal property, such as homes and tools, but organize collectively to manage public goods and critical survival needs \[cite: 1, 2\].  
* **Horizontal Organization:** Instead of top-down commands, the system matches local needs directly with local resources through peer-to-peer cooperation \[cite: 1, 2\].  
* **Direct Democracy:** The community self-governs via direct assemblies where collective decisions are made using consensus models rather than simple majority rules \[cite: 1, 2\].  
* **Voluntary Labor:** Infrastructure and maintenance are handled by voluntary work brigades formed by neighbors based on their real-world skills \[cite: 1, 2\].  
* **Community Defense:** Safety relies on de-escalation and mutual protection, utilizing unarmed, rotating neighborhood watches instead of traditional policing \[cite: 1, 2\].

## **4\. Terse Outline of Pages, Sub-Pages, and Functions**

* **1\. Home / Control Center (Default Workspace View)** \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **1.1. Emergency Broadcast System:** High-visibility tactical EMERGENCY SOS button; Direct alert dispatch interface. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **1.2. Infrastructure & Network Health Ledger:** Real-time diagnostic telemetry for the local offline Raspberry Pi mesh network server; Status mapping and traffic diagnostics of all community open wireless routers; Ping logs and topology map for localized LoRa repeaters and client nodes; Operational availability tracker and frequency assignments for Ham radio repeaters. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **1.3. Environmental Situational Awareness:** Localized weather monitoring display; Coastal tide table tracking; Solar cycle tracker showing exact sunset/sunrise times. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
* **2\. Common Fund (Resource & Logistics)** \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **2.1. Inventory Ledger:** Raw, transparent central logbook tracking physical community survival materials. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **2.2. Peer-to-Peer Mutual Aid Matcher:** "Give & Take" request board sorting neighborhood material surplus declarations against active critical supply deficits. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **2.3. Ration Allocation Calculator:** Equitable distribution utility to balance remaining inventory weights against surrounding household capacities and critical welfare variables. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
* **3\. Syndicate Task Dispatch (Labor & Repairs)** \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **3.1. Infrastructure Incident Log:** Urgent infrastructure break-fix dispatch feed (e.g., burst pipes, blocked pathways). \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **3.2. Skill Registry Cross-Referencer:** Database matching automated alerts directly to local skilled volunteers. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **3.3. Voluntary Shift Scheduler:** Distributed workforce coordinator for consistent, non-hierarchical community operations. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
* **4\. Direct Assembly (Governance & Consensus)** \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **4.1. Proposal Pipeline:** Horizontal submittal desk allowing any community resident to formulate a mutual directive requiring an initial minimum endorsement threshold. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **4.2. Consensus Indicator Panel:** Non-majoritarian "Fist-to-Five" visual polling tool designed to log consensus positions, active deferrals, or explicit structural blocks. \[cite: 1, 2\]  
  * **4.3. Mandate Log & Recall Tracker:** Enforceable accountability panel defining temporary, recallable directives for delegates negotiating with external groups. \[cite: 1, 2\]

## **5\. Footnotes and Links of Interest**

* **Hakim Bey and T.A.Z.:** The concept of the Temporary Autonomous Zone originates from Hakim Bey's 1991 book *T.A.Z.: The Temporary Autonomous Zone, Ontological Anarchy, Poetic Terrorism* \[cite: 2\]. It describes creating temporary spaces that elude formal structures of control, existing in the blind spots of the state \[cite: 2\].  
* **Mutualism and Anarcho-Syndicalism:** Political and economic philosophies where individuals maintain personal property but organize collectively to manage public goods, relying on horizontal organization rather than top-down authority \[cite: 2\].  
* **Local Mesh Networks:** To survive infrastructure collapse, communities can utilize local mesh networking via battery or solar-powered Raspberry Pi servers \[cite: 2\]. Neighbors connect to a local, password-free Wi-Fi network generated by long-range routers (like Ubiquiti) without needing broader internet access \[cite: 2\].
